KHAJURAHO TEMPLE

Description

The Khajuraho Group of Monuments is a collection of Hindu and Jain temples in Madhya Pradesh, India. The temples are known for it's Nagara style architecture, sculptures, and erotic statues.The Chandela dynasty built the temples between 950 and 1050 AD. The temples are known for their erotic sculptures.The sculptures include gods, goddesses, musicians, dancers, animals, and celestial nymphs. The name Khajuraho comes from the Sanskrit words "kharjura" (date palm) and "vahaka" (bearer).

Intersting Facts

  • The Khajuraho temples are part of the Khajuraho Group of Monuments, which was inscribed on the UNESCO World Heritage List in 1986.

  • The temples depict scenes from everyday life, such as farming, hunting, and performing arts.

  • The Matangeshwar Temple in Khajuraho has a Shivling that is believed to grow year after year.

  • The temples' design highlights Shiva, who controls the cosmic dance of time.

  • The temples were once part of a larger capital that contained about 85 temples.

Location

The Khajuraho Group of Monuments is located in the Chhatarpur district of Madhya Pradesh, India.
