HAMPI

Description

Hampi in Karnataka is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its ancient temples, monuments, and sculptures. It's also known as the "World's Largest Open-air Museum".Hampi was the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire, the last great Hindu Kingdom. Hampi's architecture is a blend of religious, royal, and civic styles.Hampi's temples feature intricate engravings of horses and yali (hippogryphs). The granite hills around Hampi are some of the oldest exposed surfaces on Earth.Hampi is set against a dramatic boulder-strewn landscape.

Hampi's highlight -

  • Virupaksha Temple:

    A 7th-century temple built on the banks of the Tungabhadra River.

  • Vittala Temple:

    A 16th-century temple with a famous stone chariot.

  • Lotus Palace:

    A remarkable landmark that was used by the royal women of the Vijayanagara Empire.

Why visit -

  • To experience the blend of religious, royal, and civic architecture.
  • To see the famous stone chariot.
  • To experience the festivals of Hampi.
  • To experience the culinary delights of Hampi.
  • To experience the vibrant atmosphere of the Hampi Bazaar.
