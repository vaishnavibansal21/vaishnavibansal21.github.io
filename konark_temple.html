KONARK SUN TEMPLE

Description

The Konark Sun Temple is a 13th-century Hindu temple in Odisha, India. It's a famous example of temple architecture and art.The temple was built by King Narasimhadeva of the Eastern Ganga dynasty around 1250 CE. The temple was once over 200 feet high, but much of it is now in ruins. It is widely known not only for its architectural grandeur but also for the intricacy and profusion of sculptural work. The temple's ruins were excavated in the late 19th century.It's a UNESCO World Heritage Centre. It's designed as a chariot with 12 wheels, 7 horses, and intricate carvings.

Intersting Facts

  • Legend says a massive lodestone at the temple's peak created a magnetic field that levitated the structure.

  • This magnetic field may have disrupted the compasses of passing ships.

  • Local lore says the main idol of Surya was suspended in mid-air due to the lodestone.

  • Local legends say the 12 chariot wheels were designed as sun dials to find time accurately to 1.5 minutes.

  • Some speculate the temple was originally located on the seashore, but was relocated inland due to a decrease in sea level.

Location

The Konark Sun Temple is located in Konark, a town in the Puri district of Odisha, India. It is on the Bay of Bengal coast.
