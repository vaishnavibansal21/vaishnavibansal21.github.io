Qutub Minar – The Tallest Brick Minaret in the World

Description

Qutub Minar, located in Delhi, India, is an architectural marvel and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Standing at 73 meters (240 feet) tall, it is the world’s tallest brick minaret and one of India’s most iconic historical.

Built in 1193 by Qutb-ud-din Aibak, the founder of the Delhi Sultanate, the Qutub Minar was constructed to celebrate the victory of Muslim rule in India.

Architectural Brilliance

  • Material: The tower is made of red sandstone and marble, showcasing intricate carvings and inscriptions from the Quran.

  • Structure: The minaret has five distinct stories, each marked by a projecting balcony.

Qutub Complex

The Qutub Minar is part of a larger Qutub Complex, which includes:

  • Quwwat-ul-Islam Mosque – The first mosque built in India.

  • Iron Pillar of Delhi – A rust-resistant iron pillar over 1,600 years old.

  • Alai Darwaza & Alai Minar – Additions by later rulers

Why Visit Qutub Minar?

Qutub Minar is a must-visit for history lovers, architecture enthusiasts, and tourists exploring Delhi. Its grandeur, historical depth, and detailed craftsmanship make it an unforgettable experience.
